Effective reading strategies

Introduction to reading

Welcome to the section of Reading!

It is important to adapt how you read to suit the material and your purpose for reading. Depending on what you are reading and why, you will find some of the following strategies useful.

Skimming

Skimming is the ability to read something very quickly and be able to note out important points. So, how do we skim?

Start to finish
By understanding the structure of the text you are reading, you will be able to read the important bits from start to finish without having to go into detail.

Start to finish
The first sentence strategy assumes that the opening sentence of each paragraph will give enough insight into what the text is about. If the text is the material you need, you can go back and re-read it in detail.

Scanning

Unlike skimming, scanning allows you to read over the whole text very quickly to find information.

Table of contents
Open the text and look at the table of contents.

Chapter headings
Read the chapter headings to see if they are relevant to the information you are looking for. If not, go to the index at the end of the text.

Index
Search the index for relevant topics or key words. If not, then look for another text that will be more fruitful for the topic.
Go to Effective researching if you need help searching.

You found something!
Go to the relevant section of the text if you have found it in the table of contents.
Go to the relevant page of the text if you have found it in the index.
