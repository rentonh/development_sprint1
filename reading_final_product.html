Effective reading strategies

Introduction to reading

Welcome to the section of Reading!

It is important to adapt how you read to suit the material and your purpose for reading. Depending on what you are reading and why, you will find some of the following strategies useful.

Skimming

Skimming is the ability to read something very quickly and be able to note out important points. So, how do we skim?

Start to finish
By understanding the structure of the text you are reading, you will be able to read the important bits from start to finish without having to go into detail.

Start to finish
The first sentence strategy assumes that the opening sentence of each paragraph will give enough insight into what the text is about. If the text is the material you need, you can go back and re-read it in detail.

Scanning

Unlike skimming, scanning allows you to read over the whole text very quickly to find information.

Table of contents
Open the text and look at the table of contents.

Chapter headings
Read the chapter headings to see if they are relevant to the information you are looking for. If not, go to the index at the end of the text.

Index
Search the index for relevant topics or key words. If not, then look for another text that will be more fruitful for the topic.
Go to Effective researching if you need help searching.

You found something!
Go to the relevant section of the text if you have found the relevant chapter heading in the table of contents.

Go to the relevant page of the text if you have found the relevant reference in the index.

Key words spotting

Key words and ideas are often embedded in a paragraph, which gives readers a good idea of what information the paragraph provides.

It also allows readers to find relevant paragraphs by looking for the key words.

To find key words, pay attention to opening sentences and opening paragraphs. Also look for hints such as underlining and bolding.

Analytical reading

Analytical reading involves reading in an active and systematic way so you understand what you are reading.

There are two steps, step 1 is to think while reading. The second step would be the SQ3R technique:

To find key words, pay attention to opening sentences and opening paragraphs. Also look for hints such as underlining and bolding.

Have a quick glance over the text, skim for key questions and key information.

Question each section

What is the main point?
Where is the evidence?
How does it explain the main point and fit in with the rest of the text?

Begin to read the text section by section.

Recall important points and note them down.

Finally, look back at the text with all the information put together and how it addressed your problems. Summarize the information and rewrite notes for easier review/reference later.

Critical reading

Critical reading involves exercising your judgement about what you are reading.

You question the text provided by the author and seek to provide answers for those questions.

Common questions include:
What is the evidence for this text?
Do I agree with it?
Is there other alternative perspectives?

Reading difficult texts

The most effective way to read a difficult text is to break the task into parts, and only work on one section at a time.

Scan the section checking headings and subheadings and look at how the text is organised.

Read the introductory and concluding paragraphs to get a general idea of what is in that part of the text.

Read the text, shorter sections at a time. Look for keywords and try to understand what the text is trying to express.
